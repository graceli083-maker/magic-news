THE ORACLE

ORACLE-8 is a satirical news dispensary operating from within the dark forest of the contemporary internet — a space so saturated with automated agents, synthetic content, and bot-generated signal that authentic information has become statistically unlikely.

Every headline dispensed by this machine is entirely fictional. Any resemblance to real events is a simulation artifact. The oracle does not lie. It simply does what the media apparatus does, faster and without pretense.

Shake the ball. Receive a dispatch. Question everything, including this sentence.

THE THEORY

This project draws on Jean Baudrillard's theory of simulation — the idea that contemporary media no longer represents reality but precedes and produces it. The generated headlines are not parodies of real news. They are structurally indistinguishable from it.

It also engages Yancey Strickler's Dark Forest Theory of the Internet: the web as a graveyard full of signals that simulate life without containing it. ORACLE-8 literalizes this condition — the joke and the critique are the same gesture.
